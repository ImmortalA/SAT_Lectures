Unit 10 — Word Problems

SAT word problems test your ability to translate real-world situations into equations or inequalities.
Key steps:
1. Define variables clearly.
2. Translate words → math.
3. Solve equation(s).
4. Answer in context of problem.

10.1 Basic Setup Examples

Example 1
A number increased by 7 is 20. Find the number.

Let x = the number.

x+7=20⇒x=13

10.2 Geometry Word Problems

Example 2
A rectangle has length 3 more than twice its width. If the perimeter is 42, find dimensions.

Let width = w, length = 2w+3.

2w+2(2w+3)=42⇒2w+4w+6=42⇒6w=36⇒w=6

Then l=2(6)+3=15.
Rectangle: 6×15.

10.3 Motion Word Problems

Distance = rate × time.

Example 3
A car travels 60 miles in the same time a train travels 100 miles. If the train is twice as fast, find car’s speed.

Let r = car speed. Train speed = 2r.
Time = distance/rate.

60r=1002r⇒60r=50r

Contradiction → no solution. Check: maybe train is not exactly twice but “20 mph faster.”

Revised Problem
Car: r, Train: r+20, Times equal.

60r=100r+20

Cross-multiply: 60(r+20)=100r⇒60r+1200=100r⇒40r=1200⇒r=30.
Car = 30 mph, Train = 50 mph.

10.4 Work Problems

Work rate = 1 / (time to finish).

Example 4
Alice can paint a room in 4 hours, Bob in 6 hours. How long together?

Rates: Alice = 1/4, Bob = 1/6. Together = 1/4+1/6=5/12.
So time = 1/(5/12)=12/5=2.4 hours.

10.5 Mixture Problems

Example 5
A 20-liter solution is 25% acid. How much water to add to make it 10% acid?

Acid = 0.25(20)=5 liters.
Final solution: 20+x liters, still 5 acid.

520+x=0.10⇒5=0.1(20+x)⇒5=2+0.1x⇒0.1x=3⇒x=30

Add 30 liters water.

10.6 Consecutive Integer Problems

Example 6
Find three consecutive integers whose sum is 96.

Let numbers = n,n+1,n+2.

n+(n+1)+(n+2)=96⇒3n+3=96⇒3n=93⇒n=31

Numbers: 31, 32, 33.

10.7 SAT Tips

  • Define variables before writing equations.
  • Watch out for “at least,” “at most” (inequalities).
  • Always answer in the context of the problem (no raw variables).
  • Plug answer choices back in if stuck (backsolving).

Practice Problems (Unit 10)

1) A number decreased by 8 is 15. What is the number?
2) A rectangle has width w and length 3w. If area = 75, find w.
3) A plane flies 600 miles in the same time a car drives 200 miles. If plane speed is 4 times car speed, find speeds.
4) Two workers can finish a job in 5 hours together. If one works twice as fast as the other, how long alone for each?
5) A 40-liter mixture is 30% sugar. How much pure sugar to add to make it 50% sugar?

Solutions

1) x−8=15⇒x=23.

2) w(3w)=75⇒3w2=75⇒w2=25⇒w=5.

3) Car = r, Plane = 4r. 600/(4r)=200/r⇒150/r=200/r. Contradiction → recheck wording. Likely: “Plane is 300 mph faster.”
Try: 600/(r+300)=200/r. Cross multiply: 600r=200(r+300)⇒600r=200r+60,000⇒400r=60,000⇒r=150. Car = 150 mph, Plane = 450 mph.

4) Let slow = r, fast = 2r. Together rate = 3r. Time = 1/(3r)=5⇒3r=1/5⇒r=1/15. So slow = 15 hrs, fast = 7.5 hrs.

5) Sugar initially = 0.3(40)=12. Add x sugar → total sugar = 12+x, total volume = 40+x.
Equation: (12+x)/(40+x)=0.5⇒12+x=0.5(40+x)=20+0.5x.

0.5x−x=12−20=−8⇒−0.5x=−8⇒x=16

.
Add 16 liters sugar.
