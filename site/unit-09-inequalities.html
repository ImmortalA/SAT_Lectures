Unit 9 — Inequalities

9.1 How to Solve Inequalities

Rules are similar to solving equations, with one key difference:
- When multiplying or dividing both sides by a negative number, the inequality sign flips.

Example 1
Solve: 3x−7<11

3x<18⇒x<6

Example 2
Solve: −2x+5≥9

−2x≥4⇒x≤−2

9.2 Inequality Word Problems

Translate carefully from words to math.

Example 3
A concert ticket costs \40.Youhaveatmost\250. How many tickets can you buy?

40x≤250⇒x≤6.25

So maximum whole tickets = 6.

Example 4
A test requires at least 70 points to pass. You currently have 42 points out of 60. How many of the remaining 40 points must you get?

Let x = points you score on last part.

42+x≥70⇒x≥28

So you must earn at least 28 points.

9.3 Graphs of Inequalities

  • For one-variable inequalities:
  • x<3 → open circle at 3, shade left.

  • x≥−2 → closed circle at -2, shade right.

  • For two-variable inequalities:

  • Example: y≤2x+1 → graph line y=2x+1. Shade below because of ≤.
  • Use dashed line for < or >; solid line for ≤ or ≥.

Example 5
Graph solution set of:

y>−x+2

Line: y=−x+2, slope −1, intercept 2. Dashed line. Shade region above.

9.4 Systems of Inequalities

The solution is the overlap region of all shaded areas.

Example 6
Graph and solve:

y≥x+1 y<−2x+4

The solution region is the intersection of the two shaded half-planes.

9.5 SAT Tips

  • Always flip the inequality when multiplying/dividing by negative.
  • Check boundary points (does equality hold?).
  • For word problems, highlight key phrases like “at most,” “at least,” “no more than.”
  • Graph shading is common on SAT — test with a sample point like (0,0).

Practice Problems (Unit 9)

1) Solve: 5x+2>17
2) Solve: −3x+7≤1
3) A taxi charges \4perrideplus\2 per mile. You have at most \$30. How many miles can you ride?
4) Solve and graph: y≤12x+3
5) System of inequalities:

y>2x−1,y≤−x+4

Solutions

1) 5x+2>17⇒5x>15⇒x>3.

2) −3x+7≤1⇒−3x≤−6⇒x≥2.

3) Cost equation: 4+2m≤30⇒2m≤26⇒m≤13. So up to 13 miles.

4) Line: y=12x+3, slope 12, intercept 3. Solid line (since ≤). Shade below.

5) Graph both: y>2x−1 (above dashed line), y≤−x+4 (below solid line). Solution = overlap region.
