Unit 6 — Manipulating & Solving Equations

6.1 Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Cancelling incorrectly
    - Wrong: x+5x=1+5=6
    - Correct: x+5x=1+5x

  2. Losing solutions when multiplying/dividing by a variable
    - If you multiply or divide by x, always check x≠0.

  3. Forgetting to distribute negatives
    - −(x+3)=−x−3, not −x+3

  4. Square root mistake
    - x2=|x|, not x

6.2 Tools for Isolating Variables

Inverse operations

  • Addition ↔ Subtraction
  • Multiplication ↔ Division
  • Squaring ↔ Square root

Example
Solve 3x+7=16

3x=9⇒x=3

Balancing equations

What you do to one side, do to the other.

6.3 How to Deal with Complicated Equations

Distribute and Combine

  • Expand parentheses and combine like terms.

Example
Solve 2(x+3)−(x−5)=12

2x+6−x+5=12⇒x+11=12⇒x=1

Fractions

  • Multiply through by least common denominator (LCD).

Example
Solve x3+25=1
Multiply both sides by 15:

5x+6=15⇒x=95

Decimals

  • Multiply through by powers of 10 to clear decimals.

Example
Solve 0.2x+0.5=1.3
Multiply through by 10:

2x+5=13⇒x=4

6.4 SAT Tips

  • Always simplify before solving.
  • Eliminate fractions and decimals early.
  • Plug solution back to check (some SAT questions have “extraneous solutions”).
  • Watch for absolute values, square roots, and restrictions.

Practice Problems (Unit 6)

1) Solve: 5x−7=18
2) Solve: 2(x−3)+4=10
3) Solve: 3x4+2=5
4) Solve: 0.1x+0.2=0.5
5) Simplify: 2x+6x

Solutions

1) 5x=25⇒x=5
2) 2x−6+4=10⇒2x−2=10⇒2x=12⇒x=6
3) 3x4=3⇒3x=12⇒x=4
4) 0.1x=0.3⇒x=3
5) 2(x+3)x=2+6x (for x≠0)
