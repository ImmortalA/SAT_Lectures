Unit 21 — Trigonometry

Trigonometry connects angles with side ratios in right triangles.
On the SAT, you’ll mainly see basic sine, cosine, tangent, and simple identities.

21.1 Sine, Cosine, and Tangent

In a right triangle:

sinθ=oppositehypotenuse,cosθ=adjacenthypotenuse,tanθ=oppositeadjacent

Example 1
In a right triangle, opposite =3, adjacent =4, hypotenuse =5:

sinθ=35,cosθ=45,tanθ=34

21.2 Trigonometric Identities

Pythagorean Identity

sin2θ+cos2θ=1

Tangent Identity

tanθ=sinθcosθ,cosθ≠0

Example 2
If sinθ=513, find cosθ.

cosθ=1−sin2θ=1−25169=1213

21.3 Evaluating Trigonometric Expressions

  • Use special right triangles (30-60-90, 45-45-90).
  • Know common values:
sin30∘=12,cos30∘=32,tan30∘=13 sin45∘=22,cos45∘=22,tan45∘=1 sin60∘=32,cos60∘=12,tan60∘=3

Example 3
Evaluate tan45∘:

tan45∘=1

21.4 SAT Tips

  • Draw the reference triangle if possible.
  • Memorize exact values for 30∘,45∘,60∘.
  • Use sin2+cos2=1 to find missing values.
  • Watch for trick questions mixing degrees and radians.
