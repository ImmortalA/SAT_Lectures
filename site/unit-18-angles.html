Unit 18 — Angles

Angles are measured in degrees (°) or radians.
Key rules involve sums of angles and relationships in geometric figures.

18.1 Exterior Angle Theorem

In a triangle, the exterior angle is equal to the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.

Exterior Angle=Remote Interior Angle 1+Remote Interior Angle 2

Example 1
In a triangle, one exterior angle is 120∘. If one remote interior angle is 40∘, the other is:

120∘−40∘=80∘

18.2 Parallel Lines and Angles

When a transversal cuts two parallel lines:

  • Corresponding angles are equal.
  • Alternate interior angles are equal.
  • Same-side interior angles are supplementary.

Example 2
If one angle is 70∘, then the corresponding angle is also 70∘.

18.3 Polygons

Interior Angle Sum

For an n-sided polygon:

Sum of interior angles=(n−2)×180∘

Each Interior Angle (regular polygon)

(n−2)×180∘n

Each Exterior Angle (regular polygon)

360∘n

Example 3
A hexagon (n=6):
- Interior angle sum =(6−2)×180=720∘
- Each interior angle =720/6=120∘

18.4 Angle Pairs

  • Complementary angles: sum =90∘
  • Supplementary angles: sum =180∘
  • Vertical angles: equal

Example 4
If two supplementary angles are in ratio 2:7, find them.

2x+7x=180⇒9x=180⇒x=20

Angles = 40∘,140∘

18.5 SAT Tips

  • Always check for parallel lines → look for equal angles.
  • For polygons, memorize (n−2)×180∘.
  • Exterior angle theorem is very common.
  • Watch out for “trick” wording with supplements/complements.

Practice Problems (Unit 18)

1) In a triangle, one angle is 50∘ and another is 60∘. Find the exterior angle adjacent to the third angle.
2) Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. If one angle is 75∘, find its alternate interior angle.
3) Find the sum of interior angles of a decagon.
4) Find each interior angle of a regular octagon.
5) Two angles are complementary. One is x, the other is 2x. Solve for x.

Solutions

1) Third angle =180−50−60=70∘. Exterior angle = 180−70=110∘.

2) Alternate interior angle = 75∘.

3) (10−2)×180=8×180=1440∘.

4) (8−2)×180/8=6×180/8=108∘.

5) x+2x=90⇒3x=90⇒x=30.
