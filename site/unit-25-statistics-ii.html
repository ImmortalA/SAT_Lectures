Unit 25 — Statistics II

This unit extends statistics into sampling, interpreting regression, and experimental design.
The SAT often tests your ability to reason about data collection and conclusions.

25.1 Statistical Sampling

  • Random Sample: Every member has equal chance of being selected → most reliable.
  • Biased Sample: Favors certain members → unreliable.

Example
Surveying only basketball players to estimate average student height = biased.
Surveying random students across school = better.

25.2 Using and Interpreting the Line of Best Fit

Scatterplots often include a regression line.

  • Positive slope: As x increases, y tends to increase.
  • Negative slope: As x increases, y tends to decrease.
  • Closer points to line: Stronger correlation.

Example
If best-fit line is y^=2x+5, then for x=4:

y^=2(4)+5=13

25.3 Margin of Error

Every estimate from a sample has some uncertainty.

  • Smaller sample size → larger margin of error.
  • Larger sample size → smaller margin of error.

Example
Poll shows 60% ± 3%. True population proportion is likely between 57% and 63%.

25.4 Confidence Intervals

A confidence interval provides a range that likely contains the true population value.

  • A 95% confidence interval means if the survey were repeated many times, 95% of the intervals would contain the true value.

Example
A survey estimates average GPA = 3.2 with 95% CI (3.0,3.4).
Interpretation: The true GPA is likely between 3.0 and 3.4.

25.5 Experimental Design and Conclusions

  • Correlation does not imply causation!
  • Randomized controlled experiments are most reliable.
  • Observational studies can show association but not cause.

Example
Data shows students who eat breakfast score higher.
Conclusion: Eating breakfast is associated with higher scores, but may not be the cause (other factors like sleep could matter).

25.6 SAT Tips

  • Look for wording like biased, random, representative.
  • Be careful: SAT may trick with causation vs. correlation.
  • For line of best fit → always use the equation given, not your own guess.
  • Interpret margin of error and confidence intervals correctly.

Practice Problems (Unit 25)

1) A school surveys only students in the honor society to estimate average study time. Is this sample biased?

2) A scatterplot shows a strong negative correlation. What does this mean?

3) A poll shows 48% support ± 4%. What is the interval of possible true support?

4) A best-fit line is y^=−3x+20. Predict y^ when x=5.

5) Researchers observe that students who text more tend to sleep less. Can they conclude texting causes less sleep?

Solutions

1) Yes — it’s biased, since only honor students are surveyed.

2) As x increases, y tends to decrease.

3) Interval = (44%,52%).

4) y^=−3(5)+20=−15+20=5.

5) No — it only shows correlation, not causation.
