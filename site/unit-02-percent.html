Unit 2 — Percent

2.1 Definition of Percent

A percent is a ratio out of 100.

Percent=PartWhole×100
  • 50% = 50100=12
  • 25% = 25100=14
  • 200% = 200100=2

2.2 Percent Change

Formula

Percent Change=New−OriginalOriginal×100%
  • Positive → increase
  • Negative → decrease

Examples
1. Price from \40to\50:

50−4040×100=25% increase
  1. Price from \80to\60:
60−8080×100=−25% decrease

2.3 Simple Interest and Compound Interest

Simple Interest

I=Prt
  • P = principal
  • r = rate (decimal)
  • t = time

Example: \1000at5I = 1000(0.05)(3)=150$

Compound Interest

A=P(1+rn)nt
  • A = total
  • P = principal
  • r = annual rate (decimal)
  • n = times compounded per year
  • t = time in years

Example: \$1000 at 5% compounded annually for 3 years

A=1000(1.05)3=1157.63

2.4 Percent Word Problems

Example 1
40% of students are girls. If there are 200 students, how many are girls?

0.40×200=80

Example 2
An item is discounted 20%, then taxed 5%. If original price is \$100:
- After discount: 100(0.8)=80
- After tax: 80(1.05)=84

2.5 Exponential vs. Linear Growth

  • Linear growth: constant addition per step
    Example: salary increases \2000eachyear→y=2000t+P$
  • Exponential growth: constant multiplication per step
    Example: bacteria doubles every hour → y=P(2t)

2.6 Linear Growth and Decay

Linear growth/decay equation:

y=y0+kt
  • k>0: growth
  • k<0: decay

Example
A tank loses 5 liters per hour, starting with 200:

y=200−5t

2.7 Exponential Growth and Decay

General formula:

y=y0(1±r)t
  • + for growth
  • − for decay

Example
Car worth \$20,000 depreciates 10% per year:

y=20000(0.9)t

2.8 Positive and Negative Association

  • Positive association: as x increases, y tends to increase.
  • Negative association: as x increases, y tends to decrease.

Examples - Hours studied vs. test score → positive
- Speed vs. travel time → negative
