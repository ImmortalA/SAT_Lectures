Unit 12 — Interpreting Linear Models

On the SAT, you’ll often see real-world word problems with a linear model given in the form:

y=mx+b

where
- m= slope (rate of change)
- b= intercept (starting value)

You must interpret what m and b mean in context.

12.1 Meaning of the Slope

  • Slope is the change in y per unit change in x.
  • Interpreted as a rate.

Example 1
A taxi company charges according to the equation:

Cost=2.5x+4

where x = miles traveled.

  • Slope = 2.5 → cost increases by \$2.50 per mile.
  • Intercept = 4 → flat starting fee = \$4.

12.2 Meaning of the Intercept

  • Intercept is the value of y when x=0.
  • Often represents initial condition.

Example 2
Temperature T=70−2t, where t = hours after noon.

  • Intercept = 70 → temperature at noon = 70°F.
  • Slope = -2 → temperature drops 2°F each hour.

12.3 Predictions with Linear Models

You can use the equation to predict values.

Example 3
A company’s revenue is modeled by:

R=5000+200t

where t = months.

  • After 6 months:
R=5000+200(6)=6200

So revenue = \$6200.

12.4 Limitations of Models

  • Models may not apply for all x.
  • Negative values may be meaningless (e.g., negative time, negative price).
  • Always check context.

Example 4
If h=100−5t gives height of rocket (t= seconds), then at t=30:

h=100−150=−50

Negative height is meaningless → model only valid for small t.

12.5 Scatterplots & Line of Best Fit

On the SAT:
- A line of best fit is given.
- You may be asked to interpret slope/intercept.
- Or check if a point fits the model.

Example 5
Scatterplot shows relation between study hours (x) and test score (y). Line of best fit:

y=50+5x
  • Intercept = 50 → baseline score with 0 study = 50.
  • Slope = 5 → each study hour adds 5 points.

12.6 SAT Tips

  • Identify units for slope/intercept.
  • Watch for unrealistic predictions.
  • If a graph/table is shown, pick two points to compute slope.
  • Double-check whether the model describes growth or decline.

Practice Problems (Unit 12)

1) A cell phone plan costs C=40+0.1m, where m = number of minutes. Interpret slope and intercept.

2) A car’s value is modeled as V=20,000−1200y, where y = years since purchase. What does slope mean?

3) A fitness app predicts weight loss with W=180−2t, where t = weeks. Find weight after 5 weeks.

4) The line of best fit for data is y=30+4x. Interpret slope in context.

5) A model predicts y=100−15x. For x=10, what is y? Is this prediction reasonable?

Solutions

1) Intercept = 40 → fixed monthly fee \40.Slope=0.1→eachminuteadds\0.10.

2) Slope = -1200 → car loses \$1200 value each year.

3) W=180−2(5)=170. After 5 weeks, weight = 170 lbs.

4) Slope = 4 → each increase of 1 unit in x raises y by 4.

5) y=100−15(10)=100−150=−50. Prediction meaningless (negative). Model not valid for large x.
