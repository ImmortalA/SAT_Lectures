3.1 Solving Linear Equations

A linear equation has the general form:

ax+b=c

Steps:

  1. Move variable terms to one side.
  2. Move constants to the other.
  3. Divide by the coefficient of x.

Example

3x−5=16⇒3x=21⇒x=7

3.2 Equations with Variables on Both Sides

Example

5x+2=3x+8⇒5x−3x=8−2⇒2x=6⇒x=3

SAT Tip: Always combine like terms before solving.

3.3 Equations with Fractions

Example

x3+x6=5

Multiply through by the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators:

6(x3+x6)=6(5)⇒2x+x=30⇒3x=30⇒x=10

3.4 Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation has the form:

ax2+bx+c=0

Methods:

  1. Factoring (when easy integers exist)
    Example:
x2+5x+6=0(x+2)(x+3)=0x=−2,x=−3
  1. Quadratic Formula
x=−b±b2−4ac2a
  1. Completing the Square
    Turn x2+bx into (x+b2)2 form.

3.5 Absolute Value Equations

Definition:

|x|=k⇒x=k or x=−k

Example

|2x−3|=5⇒2x−3=5or2x−3=−5⇒x=4orx=−1

SAT Tip: Always check for extraneous solutions in absolute value and radical equations.

3.6 Radical Equations

Equations with square roots:

Example

x+3=x−1
  1. Square both sides:
x+3=(x−1)2
  1. Expand:
x+3=x2−2x+1
  1. Rearrange:
0=x2−3x−2
  1. Factor:
(x−2)(x−(−1))=0x=2orx=−1
  1. Check: Plug into original equation — x=−1 gives 2=−2 (false), so only x=2 works.

3.7 Systems of Equations

Two main methods on the SAT:

  1. Substitution - Solve one equation for one variable, substitute into the other.

  2. Elimination - Add/subtract equations to eliminate one variable.

Example

{x+y=102x−y=4

Add both:

3x=14 ⇒ x=143

Substitute into x+y=10:

143+y=10 ⇒ y=163
