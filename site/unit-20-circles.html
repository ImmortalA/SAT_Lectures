Unit 20 — Circles

Circles are a major SAT topic. You’ll see questions on circumference, area, arc length, sectors, angles, and equations.

20.1 Area and Circumference

  • Circumference:
C=2πr=πd
  • Area:
A=πr2

Example 1
If r=7, then:

C=14π,A=49π

20.2 Arc Length

Arc length is a portion of the circumference:

s=θ360∘·2πr

(or s=θr if θ in radians).

Example 2
Find the arc length of a 90∘ arc in a circle of radius 4.

s=90360·2π(4)=2π

20.3 Area of a Sector

Sector area is a portion of the total area:

Asector=θ360∘·πr2

(or Asector=12r2θ if θ in radians).

Example 3
Find the area of a 60∘ sector with r=6:

A=60360·π(36)=6π

20.4 Central and Inscribed Angles

  • Central angle: vertex at the circle’s center, equals arc measure.
  • Inscribed angle: vertex on the circle, equals half the arc it intercepts.

Example 4
If arc AB=80∘, then:
- Central angle =80∘
- Inscribed angle =40∘

20.5 Equations of Circles

General form:

(x−h)2+(y−k)2=r2

where (h,k) is the center and r the radius.

Example 5
Equation of circle centered at (3,−2) with r=5:

(x−3)2+(y+2)2=25

20.6 SAT Tips

  • Inscribed angle = half arc is very common.
  • For arc/sector problems, always check if θ is in degrees or radians.
  • If you see x2+y2 terms, think “equation of a circle.”
  • Don’t forget diameter =2r when needed.

Practice Problems (Unit 20)

1) Find the circumference of a circle with r=10.
2) Find the area of a circle with diameter 12.
3) In a circle of radius 5, find the length of an arc subtended by 72∘.
4) Find the area of a 90∘ sector with r=8.
5) In a circle, arc AB=100∘. Find the inscribed angle subtending that arc.
6) Write the equation of a circle centered at (2,3) with radius 7.

Solutions

1) C=2π(10)=20π.

2) r=6⇒A=π(36)=36π.

3) s=72360·2π(5)=2π.

4) A=90360·π(64)=16π.

5) Inscribed angle =100/2=50∘.

6) (x−2)2+(y−3)2=49.
