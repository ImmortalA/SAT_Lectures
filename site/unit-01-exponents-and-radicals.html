Unit 1 — Exponents & Radicals

1.1 Laws of Exponents

For a≠0 and integers m,n:

  1. am·an=am+n
  2. aman=am−n
  3. (am)n=amn
  4. (ab)m=ambm
  5. (ab)m=ambm
  6. a0=1
  7. a−m=1am

Examples
- x3·x5=x8
- y7y2=y5
- (2x)3=8x3
- x−4=1x4

1.2 Evaluating Expressions with Exponents

  • Substitute the value of the base and apply exponent rules.
  • Negative bases behave differently depending on exponent parity.

Examples
- 23=8
- (−2)4=16 (even power → positive)
- (−2)5=−32 (odd power → negative)
- 2−3=18

1.3 Solving Equations with Exponents

Case 1: Same base

Rewrite with the same base, then equate exponents.

Example

3x+1=35⇒x+1=5⇒x=4

Case 2: Different bases

Take logs when bases cannot be matched.

Example

2x=7⇒x=ln7ln2

1.4 Simplifying Square Roots

Key rules

  • ab=a·b
  • ab=ab,b>0
  • (a)2=a

Examples
- 50=25·2=52
- 728=728=9=3

Rationalizing the denominator

Multiply top and bottom by the radical to remove from denominator.

Example

32·22=322

Practice Problems (Unit 1)

1) Simplify: x7y−3x−2y4
2) Evaluate: (−3)4 and −34 (note parentheses).
3) Solve for x: 52x−1=125
4) Simplify: 98
5) Rationalize: 75

Solutions

1) x7−(−2)y−3−4=x9y−7=x9y7
2) (−3)4=81, but −34=−81
3) 125=53⇒2x−1=3⇒x=2
4) 98=49·2=72
5) 75·55=755
