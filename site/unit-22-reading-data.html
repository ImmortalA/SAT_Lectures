Unit 22 — Reading Data

Data interpretation questions test your ability to understand tables, graphs, and charts.
The SAT focuses on extracting numerical information and making comparisons.

22.1 Types of Data Representations

  • Tables: Organize data into rows and columns.
  • Bar Graphs: Compare discrete categories.
  • Line Graphs: Show trends over time.
  • Pie Charts: Show proportions of a whole.
  • Scatterplots: Show relationships between two variables.

22.2 Common Skills

Finding Totals

Add values across rows or columns.

Example 1
A table shows that 20 boys and 15 girls joined the math club.

Total=20+15=35

Percentages

Convert part-to-whole into percent.

Example 2
In a survey, 30 students like soccer, 10 like basketball.

Soccer percent=3040×100=75%

Ratios

Express comparisons in simplest form.

Example 3
If a pie chart shows 120 students: 90 prefer cats, 30 prefer dogs.

Ratio cats:dogs=9030=3:1

Interpreting Trends

Look at increases/decreases in line graphs.

  • Rising line → positive trend.
  • Falling line → negative trend.
  • Flat line → no change.

22.3 SAT Tips

  • Read axes carefully (units often trick students).
  • Watch for scales not starting at 0.
  • Double-check if a question asks for percent vs. raw number.
  • Ratios are part-to-part, percents are part-to-whole.

Practice Problems (Unit 22)

1) A bar graph shows sales: Monday 40, Tuesday 60, Wednesday 50.
- What is the average daily sales?

2) A pie chart shows 200 students: 50 like math, 75 like science, rest like history.
- What percent like history?

3) A line graph shows company revenue: Year 1 = 100k, Year 2 = 120k, Year 3 = 90k.
- What is the overall percent change from Year 1 to Year 3?

4) A scatterplot shows a positive association.
- What does this mean about the two variables?

Solutions

1) Average = (40+60+50)/3=150/3=50.

2) History =200−(50+75)=75.

75200×100=37.5%

3) Change =90k−100k=−10k.

−10k100k×100=−10%

4) As one variable increases, the other tends to increase.
