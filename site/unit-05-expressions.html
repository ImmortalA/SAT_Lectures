Unit 5 — Expressions

5.1 Combining Like Terms

  • Like terms have the same variable raised to the same power.
  • Only their coefficients can be added or subtracted.

Examples - 3x+5x=8x
- 7y−2y=5y
- 2x2+3x−x2+4=(2x2−x2)+3x+4=x2+3x+4

5.2 Expansion and Factoring

Expansion

Use distributive property:

a(b+c)=ab+ac

Examples - (x+2)(x+3)=x2+5x+6
- (2x−5)(3x+4)=6x2−7x−20

Factoring

Reverse of expansion.
- Take out common factor.
- Use special patterns:

  1. Difference of squares:
a2−b2=(a−b)(a+b)
  1. Perfect square trinomials:
a2+2ab+b2=(a+b)2 a2−2ab+b2=(a−b)2

Examples - x2+7x+10=(x+5)(x+2)
- 9x2−25=(3x−5)(3x+5)

5.3 Combining, Dividing, and Splitting Fractions

Adding/Subtracting

Must have common denominator.

ab+cd=ad+bcbd

Example

23+56=46+56=96=32

Multiplying

Multiply numerator and denominator directly.

ab·cd=acbd

Dividing

Multiply by reciprocal.

ab÷cd=ab·dc

Splitting Fractions

Distribute denominator:

a+bc=ac+bc

Example

x+63=x3+2

5.4 SAT Tips

  • Always factor before canceling terms in fractions.
  • Look for special products: (a+b)2, (a−b)2, a2−b2.
  • Simplify step by step; avoid canceling across addition.
  • Expressions with radicals or fractions often simplify after factoring.
