Unit 4 — Rates

4.1 Definition

A rate compares two different quantities with different units.

Rate=Quantity AQuantity B

Examples - Speed = distancetime
- Density = massvolume
- Price per item = costnumber of items

4.2 Unit Rates

A unit rate expresses the rate per 1 unit.

Examples - 180 miles in 3 hours → 1803=60 miles/hour
- \12for4poundsofapples→\dfrac{12}{4} = 3$ dollars/pound

4.3 Conversion Factors

A conversion factor is a fraction equal to 1, used to change units.

Common examples - 1 hour=60 minutes
- 1 inch=2.54 cm
- 1 mile=5280 ft

Example
Convert 5 hours to minutes:

5 hr·60 min1 hr=300 min

4.4 Distance–Rate–Time Formula

Distance=Rate×Time
  • d=rt
  • r=dt
  • t=dr

Example
If speed is 50 mph for 3 hours:

d=50×3=150 miles

4.5 Word Problems

Example 1: Travel
A car travels 180 miles in 3 hours. What is its speed?

r=1803=60 mph

Example 2: Conversion
How many seconds in 2 hours?

2 hr·60 min1 hr·60 s1 min=7200 s

Example 3: Combined Rates
If Pipe A fills a tank in 4 hours, Pipe B fills the same tank in 6 hours, how long if both work together?
Rates: A=14, B=16. Combined = 14+16=512.
Time = 125=2.4 hours.

4.6 SAT Tips

  • Always include units — many traps involve mismatched units.
  • For combined rates, add rates, not times.
  • For ratios in word problems, set up proportions.
  • Convert early to avoid mistakes (miles → feet, hours → minutes).
