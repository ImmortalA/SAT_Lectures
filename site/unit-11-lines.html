Unit 11 — Lines

Understanding lines is crucial for SAT math because they appear in coordinate geometry, word problems, and graphs.

11.1 Slope and y-Intercept

  • Slope (m): measures steepness.
m=riserun=y2−y1x2−x1
  • y-intercept (b): point where line crosses y-axis.

Equation form:

y=mx+b

Example 1
Find slope and intercept of line through (2,3) and (4,7).

m=7−34−2=42=2

Equation: y=2x+b. Plug (2,3): 3=2(2)+b⇒3=4+b⇒b=−1.
Final: y=2x−1.

11.2 Equations of Lines

Slope-Intercept Form

y=mx+b

Point-Slope Form

y−y1=m(x−x1)

Example 2
Equation of line through (1,2) with slope −3:

y−2=−3(x−1)⇒y=−3x+5

11.3 Finding the Intersection of Two Lines

Solve system of equations.

Example 3
Find intersection of y=2x+1 and y=−x+4.

Set equal:

2x+1=−x+4⇒3x=3⇒x=1

Then y=2(1)+1=3.
Intersection = (1,3).

11.4 Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

  • Parallel lines: same slope, different intercepts.
  • Perpendicular lines: slopes multiply to −1 (negative reciprocals).

Example 4
Find equation of line parallel to y=3x+2 through (0,5).

Slope = 3. Equation: y=3x+5.

Example 5
Find equation of line perpendicular to y=12x−1 through (2,0).

Slope = −1(12)=−2.
Equation: y−0=−2(x−2)⇒y=−2x+4.

11.5 Horizontal and Vertical Lines

  • Horizontal: y=c (slope = 0).
  • Vertical: x=c (slope undefined).

Example 6
Equation of line through (3,−2) and parallel to y=−2:
Answer: y=−2.

Example 7
Equation of line through (4,5) and parallel to x=1:
Answer: x=4.

11.6 SAT Tips

  • Memorize slope formulas.
  • Parallel → same m.
  • Perpendicular → m1·m2=−1.
  • Watch out for vertical/horizontal lines.
  • For intersections, substitution is often faster than graphing.

Practice Problems (Unit 11)

1) Find slope and intercept of line through (0,4) and (2,10).
2) Write equation of line with slope 5 through (1,−3).
3) Find intersection of y=3x+1 and y=−2x+6.
4) Write equation of line parallel to y=−4x+7 through (2,1).
5) Write equation of line perpendicular to y=2x−5 through (−1,2).

Solutions

1) m=(10−4)/(2−0)=6/2=3. Equation: y=3x+4.

2) Point-slope: y+3=5(x−1)⇒y=5x−8.

3) 3x+1=−2x+6⇒5x=5⇒x=1,y=4. Intersection (1,4).

4) Parallel slope −4. Equation: y−1=−4(x−2)⇒y=−4x+9.

5) Perpendicular slope −12. Equation: y−2=−12(x+1)⇒y=−12x+32.
