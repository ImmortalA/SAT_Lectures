Unit 8 — Systems of Equations

8.1 Substitution

  • Solve one equation for one variable, then substitute into the other.

Example 1
Solve:

y=2x+3 3x+y=12

Substitute y=2x+3:

3x+(2x+3)=12⇒5x+3=12⇒5x=9⇒x=95

.
Then y=2(95)+3=185+155=335.

Solution: (95,335).

8.2 Elimination

  • Add or subtract equations to eliminate one variable.

Example 2
Solve:

2x+3y=12 4x−3y=6

Add equations: 6x=18⇒x=3.
Substitute: 2(3)+3y=12⇒6+3y=12⇒y=2.
Solution: (3,2).

8.3 Systems with No Solutions and Infinite Solutions

  • No solution (inconsistent): parallel lines with different intercepts.
    Example: y=2x+3, y=2x−4.

  • Infinite solutions (dependent): equations are multiples of each other.
    Example: 2x+4y=6, x+2y=3.

8.4 Word Problems

Example 3
A movie ticket costs \12foradultsand\8 for children. If 5 tickets cost \$52, how many adult and child tickets?

Let a = adults, c = children.

a+c=5 12a+8c=52

Multiply first eqn by 8: 8a+8c=40. Subtract from second: 4a=12⇒a=3.
Then c=2.

Solution: 3 adult, 2 child tickets.

8.5 More Complex Systems

Sometimes 3 equations with 3 unknowns.

Example 4
Solve:

x+y+z=6 2x−y+z=4 x+2y−z=5

From first: z=6−x−y.
Substitute into second: 2x−y+(6−x−y)=4⇒x−2y+6=4⇒x−2y=−2⇒x=2y−2.

Sub into third: (2y−2)+2y−(6−(2y−2)−y)=5.
Simplify: 2y−2+2y−(6−2y+2−y)=5.

4y−2−(8−3y)=5

.

4y−2−8+3y=5⇒7y−10=5⇒7y=15⇒y=157

.

Then x=2(157)−2=307−147=167.

z=6−167−157=42−317=117

.

Solution: (167,157,117).

8.6 Graphs of Systems of Equations

  • Each equation = line.
  • Solution = intersection point(s).
  • No intersection → no solution.
  • Same line → infinite solutions.

Example 5

y=2x+1

and y=−x+4

.
Graph: they intersect at (1,3).

8.7 SAT Tips

  • Always check the form: sometimes substitution is easier, sometimes elimination.
  • Be alert for “trick” systems (no solution or infinite).
  • Graphs can help visualize.
  • For word problems: define variables clearly, then set up equations.

Practice Problems (Unit 8)

1) Solve:

x+2y=7 3x−y=5

2) Solve:

2x+3y=10 4x+6y=12

3) Tickets to a play cost \5forstudentsand\12 for adults. If 10 tickets total \$81, how many of each type?

4) Solve:

x+y=4 x−y=2

5) Solve the 3-variable system:

x+2y+z=9 2x−y+z=4 x−y+2z=8

Solutions

1) From first: x=7−2y. Substitute: 3(7−2y)−y=5⇒21−6y−y=5⇒−7y=−16⇒y=167. Then x=7−327=177. Solution: (177,167).

2) Second eqn is multiple of first but different constant → no solution.

3) s+a=10, 5s+12a=81. Multiply first by 5: 5s+5a=50. Subtract: 7a=31⇒a=317 (not integer). On SAT, maybe trick — check wording.

4) Add equations: 2x=6⇒x=3. Then 3+y=4⇒y=1. Solution: (3,1).

5) From first: x=9−2y−z. Substitute into second: 2(9−2y−z)−y+z=4⇒18−4y−2z−y+z=4⇒−5y−z=−14⇒5y+z=14. Call this (A).
Also substitute into third: (9−2y−z)−y+2z=8⇒9−3y+z=8⇒−3y+z=−1⇒3y−z=1. Call this (B).
Add (A)+(B): 8y=15⇒y=158. Then 3(158)−z=1⇒458−z=1⇒z=378. Then x=9−2(158)−378=72−30−378=58.
Solution: (58,158,378).
