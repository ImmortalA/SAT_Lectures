Unit 7 — More Equation Solving Strategies

7.1 Matching Coefficients

Useful when equations are polynomials or involve unknown coefficients.

Example 1
Suppose (ax+b)(x+2)=2x2+7x+6.
Expand LHS: ax2+(2a+b)x+2b.
Match coefficients with RHS:
- a=2
- 2a+b=7⇒4+b=7⇒b=3
- 2b=6 (checks out)

So a=2,b=3.

7.2 Clearing Denominators

For equations with fractions, multiply both sides by the least common denominator (LCD).

Example 2
Solve x2+34=5.
LCD = 4. Multiply through:

2x+3=20⇒2x=17⇒x=172

Example 3
Solve 2x+3x+1=1.
LCD = x(x+1). Multiply through:

2(x+1)+3x=x(x+1) 2x+2+3x=x2+x⇒x2−4x−2=0

Solve quadratic:

x=4±16+82=4±242=2±6

7.3 SAT Tips

  • Always check for restrictions when clearing denominators (denominators ≠0).
  • Matching coefficients often appears in SAT questions with polynomial identities.
  • After clearing denominators, check for extraneous solutions by plugging back.

Practice Problems (Unit 7)

1) Solve for a and b: (ax+b)(x+1)=3x2+7x+2
2) Solve: x3+56=4
3) Solve: 1x+1x+2=34
4) Solve: 2y−1y+3=1

Solutions

1) Expand LHS: ax2+(a+b)x+b. Match with RHS 3x2+7x+2:
- a=3
- a+b=7⇒3+b=7⇒b=4
- b=2 conflict! → no solution.

2) Multiply through by 6: 2x+5=24⇒2x=19⇒x=192

3) LCD = x(x+2): (x+2)+x=34(x2+2x)
Simplify: 2x+2=34(x2+2x)
Multiply by 4: 8x+8=3x2+6x

3x2−2x−8=0

Solve: x=2±4+966=2±106
→ x=2 or x=−43

4) LCD = y(y+3): 2(y+3)−y=y(y+3)

2y+6−y=y2+3y

y+6=y2+3y y2+2y−6=0

Solve: y=−2±4+242=−2±282=−2±272
→ y=−1±7
